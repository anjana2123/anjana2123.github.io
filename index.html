About Me

My journey in technology began with a deeply personal experience - losing my paternal grandmother to liver cancer. Initially determined to become a doctor to serve healthcare directly, the COVID-19 pandemic transformed my perspective on how I could make the greatest impact.

Witnessing the healthcare system's challenges during the pandemic, I discovered the transformative power of AI and data science in healthcare. This revelation led me to pursue Artificial Intelligence and Data Science, where I could enhance healthcare systems through technology rather than just being part of them.

Currently, I'm pursuing my Master's in AI Engineering - Biomedical Engineering at Carnegie Mellon University, where I'm a Research Assistant at the RCHI (Robot-Computer-Human Interaction) Lab in the Robotics Institute. My research focuses on developing AI-driven solutions for precision medicine, computational oncology, and healthcare innovation.
